# Binding legal and licensing constraints

Read this file before changing any feature named below or introducing an adjacent implementation. The detailed claim and compatibility records live in [smart-objects.md](smart-objects.md), [selection-tools.md](selection-tools.md), [liquify.md](liquify.md), [brushes.md](brushes.md), [vector-tools.md](vector-tools.md), and [file-formats.md](file-formats.md). These constraints are binding even when a broader feature appears technically straightforward.

## Selection tools

- **Quick Select solves once on mouse release.** Do not add live per-mouse-move classification or a selection preview before November 3, 2029 (Adobe US 8050498). Enhance Edge remains geometric, the solve uses one window per stroke, and input never selects among algorithms automatically. `ui_quick_select_stroke_selects_object_and_is_undoable` enforces the no-mid-drag behavior.
- Magnetic Lasso and brush shape-dynamics patents are expired, so live snap display is allowed. Magnetic Lasso still constructs its selection region once in `finish_magnetic_lasso()`, clearly separate from Quick Select's prohibited live region classification.

## Filters and Smart Objects

- Smart Objects, Smart Filters, and Filter Gallery architecture are cleared subject to the design boundaries below.
- **Surface Blur may not compute through a value histogram.** Median and Dust & Scratches keep exactly one window histogram updated one pixel value at a time; never introduce column, merged, or slid histograms before the relevant expirations (US 8594445 and US 7920741). The calibrated outputs and constraint comments in `smart_filter_renderer.cpp` are binding.
- **Tilt-Shift Blur uses short grip bars.** Never restore lines spanning or dividing the image before 2033 (Apple US 8971623). The algorithm itself is clear.
- **Lens Blur and Iris Blur remain fixed and geometric.** Lens Blur is one explicitly selected aperture convolution with no depth inference, classification, highlight detection, or per-pixel kernel. Iris Blur computes one round blur and blends it through one explicit ellipse. Do not add multiple pins, combined blur patterns, Photoshop-style iris boundary widgets, variable-radius kernels, bokeh thresholds, learned depth/focal mattes, splatting, or native `blurbTransform` authoring without a new review (US 8831371, US 9019310, US 8824793, US 2025/0104196). Imported Blur Gallery stacks remain byte-preserved and preview-locked; Patchy's Lens/Iris filters remain raster-only.
- **Liquify remains manual, generic, and raster-only.** Never add face detection, landmarks, automatic face regions, face-feature controls, or deformation derived from a detected face without a new review (US 9646195). Do not invent or partially author Photoshop's native Liquify Smart Filter descriptor. Ordinary layers save the raster result, Smart Objects require rasterization, and imported unsupported stacks remain byte-preserved.
- **Plastic Wrap keeps its fixed local height-field formula:** integer alpha-weighted luminance, box smoothing, gradient, and relief. Do not add material or lighting inference, classification, patch search or synthesis, or content-driven algorithm selection. Its PSD surface is the native three-integer `PlsW` descriptor; Patchy's renderer preserves bounds and alpha without claiming Photoshop-identical pixels.
- **Unsharp Mask and Motion Blur remain fixed and user-directed.** Unsharp Mask uses one radius-selected low-pass plus explicit Amount and Threshold. Motion Blur uses one straight-line kernel from explicit Angle and Distance. Do not add ranking, inferred blur, automatic thresholds, patch search, deconvolution, shake estimation, tracking, optical flow, content-adaptive kernels, per-pixel paths, or Blur Gallery-style widgets without a new review.
- **Future feature gates:** no content-aware fill, patch-based healing, or reshuffle-style synthesis before the PatchMatch family expires (US 8285055, US 8340463, US 8355592, around 2031). No ML/GAN dust-and-scratch removal while the US 11393077 family is active. Re-read US 12530821 before integrating generative output into Smart Filter stacks. Never create a one-click merge-visible plus effects plus masked-copy operation without re-reading US 8655112. Classic user-directed healing remains allowed.

## Brush behavior

- **Flow and Airbrush remain flat-stamp operations.** They may repeat the current 2D stamp at fixed spatial/time cadences and cap accumulated paint at Opacity. Do not add particles, a 3D spray cone, stylus-pose projection, velocity-dependent deposition, bristle or fluid simulation, wet/dirty paint, or bidirectional transfer without re-reading US 8854342 and the US 9600907/10489937 family.
- **Brush Texture is one static world-anchored grayscale mask.** Pressure, velocity, direction, rotation, and stylus pose may not select, compose, or weight texture channels while US 10902645 and US 8896579 are active.
- **Color Dynamics** only interpolates the two selected colors and applies direct HSV offsets. It may not sample the canvas, mix paint, or build a 3D RGB Bezier path (US 10924633).
- **Wet Edges** keeps one maximum-coverage union mask per stroke with no canvas-color feedback, paint store, fluid, pigment, paper, drying, or pickup simulation.
- **Dual Brush** remains one fixed secondary computed mask, never a component graph or hierarchy.
- **Mixer Brush** reads one active-layer RGBA pixel at mouse-down and does not sample again during the stroke. Do not add repeated pickup, contamination stores, per-bristle/per-pixel paint state, fill channels, erodible height-map tips, fluid/drying/cleaning simulation, or 3D RGB curves without reviewing US 7768525, US 8749572, US 10217253, and US 10924633. ABR input-driven texture depth imports as a static depth with a notice.
- **Pattern Stamp** performs a direct lookup into one static tile. It may not inspect the destination for illumination, gradients, shading, or reflections; adapt or synthesize the pattern from content; or assemble runtime texture channels driven by pressure, direction, velocity, or orientation (US 9864922 and US 10902645).

## Format, copyright, trademarks, and assets

- PSD interoperability is based on observed output from a licensed Photoshop, never decompilation. Do not copy verbatim Adobe specification text into the repository; link to it. Use self-authored fixtures only and do not support the undocumented PSDC cloud-document format.
- "Smart Object(s)", "Smart Filter(s)", "Filter Gallery", and "PSD" are not Adobe trademarks in the reviewed record. PHOTOSHOP is registered and policed. Use referential language such as "compatible with Adobe Photoshop"; never use Adobe marks in Patchy's product name, icons, or branding, and retain README.md's Trademark Notice.
- Bundled art and textures must never come from AI image generation. Acceptable sources are code generation or real photography/human artwork under a commercially compatible license, preferably CC0. Every third-party asset needs a `NOTICE-THIRD-PARTY.md` entry and must ship losslessly. For ambientCG use only photogrammetry/photo `creationMethod`; Poly Haven textures are human-made.
- HEIC/HEIF decoding uses platform codecs only. Never vendor libheif, libde265, x265, or another HEVC decoder/encoder, and do not add HEIC encoding. See [file-formats.md](file-formats.md) for the patent-pool rationale and platform design.
- Shipping dependencies must permit commercial distribution. GPL tools may be used locally for development or fixture generation only when they are not linked into or distributed with Patchy.
- No Photoshop SDK code or Adobe-created assets may enter this repository or a Patchy binary. The only exception is the self-authored regression material in `test-fixtures/psd/`, which is not Adobe artwork.
